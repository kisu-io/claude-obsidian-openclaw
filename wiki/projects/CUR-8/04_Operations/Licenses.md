Critical path
PCCC (#6) is the single longest-lead item and is the most likely to push opening. PCCC requires the building's fire system to be physically installed and tested before inspection — meaning PCCC application happens in week 6–7 of construction at the earliest.
ATVSTP (#3) requires the kitchen to be physically installed and equipped. Apply in week 7.
Liquor (#4) requires ATVSTP first. Apply week 8.

Risks

PCCC change requests are the #1 reason HCMC F&B venues miss opening dates. Budget for 1–2 rounds.
ATVSTP can require kitchen layout changes — engage consultant early so the design-stage layout is already compliant.
Liquor + music sometimes require physical inspection — sequence to fit.
Outdoor / rooftop permit is the most variable — district committees vary in process.

Owner
GM owns licensing end-to-end. Recommended: hire a local licensing facilitator at week 1 — saves 2–3 weeks of trial-and-error.
